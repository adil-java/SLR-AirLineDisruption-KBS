# Research Questions

## PCC (Population, Concept, Context) Mapping

This review uses the PCC framework, appropriate for methodological and
architectural reviews where no comparator group is defined.

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Population (P)** | Airline Operations Control Centres (AOCCs) and airline operations management systems handling Irregular Operations (IROPS) — including scenarios involving flight delays, cancellations, aircraft mechanical failures, crew unavailability, adverse weather, and air traffic control restrictions |
| **Concept (C)** | Knowledge-Based Systems, Expert Systems, Decision Support Systems, optimisation models, and intelligent recovery planning frameworks applied to airline disruption management and recovery planning — including aircraft rerouting, crew rescheduling, passenger recovery, and integrated multi-resource recovery |
| **Context (C)** | Real-time or near-real-time operational decision-making within the airline industry from 2001 to 2025, with emphasis on architectures incorporating explicit knowledge representation, rule-based reasoning, constraint satisfaction, or hybrid intelligent approaches |

## Research Questions

**RQ1:** What algorithmic techniques, optimisation methods, and knowledge
representation paradigms are currently utilised for airline disruption
management and recovery planning?

**RQ2:** What are the prevalent architectural patterns, reasoning flows, and
system components defining existing decision support and knowledge-based
systems in the airline operations domain?

**RQ3:** How are structured operational knowledge sources, constraint models,
and domain expertise integrated with underlying computational engines to
support real-time disruption recovery?

**RQ4:** What are the primary limitations and research gaps in current airline
disruption management systems regarding integration, explainability,
passenger-centricity, and real-time responsiveness?

## Justification of question framework choice

PCC (rather than PICO) is used because the research is fundamentally
architectural and methodological — primary studies present systems, algorithms,
and frameworks rather than comparing interventions on a clinical or experimental
population. This follows PRISMA 2020 item 4 which permits alternative frameworks
where appropriate to the review type.
